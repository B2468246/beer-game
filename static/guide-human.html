Manual Play Mode

How to Play

You control the orders. Every round, you decide how much beer to order — based on real demand, your stock, and your gut feeling.

2 Wait in the Lobby

You'll see a waiting screen while other students join. The professor starts the game once everyone is in.

When the game starts, you're automatically assigned to a team of 4 and given one (or two) roles in the supply chain:

Retailer

Sells beer to customers. Faces real consumer demand.

Wholesaler

Supplies the Retailer. Sees only the Retailer's orders.

Distributor

Supplies the Wholesaler. One more step removed from the customer.

Manufacturer

Produces the beer. Furthest from real demand — hardest to predict.

Odd number of players? Some players get two roles and will see two order cards each round.
3

3 The Supply Chain

Beer flows from the Factory through the chain to the Customer. Orders flow the other way. Each shipment takes 2 weeks to arrive (the "lead time").

5 Watch the Round Play Out

Once all players have submitted, the round processes automatically. Trucks animate across the game board delivering beer along the supply chain.

A new order panel appears for the next round. Repeat until all rounds are complete.

Costs per Week
Holding: €0.50 per unit in stock
Backlog: €1.00 per unit owed

Your goal: minimize total cost. Too much stock is expensive — but running out is worse.

The trap: Because shipments take 2 weeks, you won't see the effect of your order immediately. It's tempting to over-order when backlog builds up — but that's exactly how the Bullwhip Effect starts.
6

6 The Goal

Keep costs low. Every unit sitting in your warehouse costs €0.50/week. Every unit of backlog costs €1.00/week.

The winning strategy? Stay calm. Order steadily. Don't panic when demand changes — your pipeline is already bringing supplies.
