2 Teams & Roles

When the professor starts, you're assigned to a team of 4 with a role in the supply chain. Each role buys from the one above it:

Retailer

Sells beer to customers. Faces real consumer demand.

Wholesaler

Supplies the Retailer. Sees only the Retailer's orders.

Distributor

Supplies the Wholesaler. One more step removed from the customer.

Manufacturer

Produces the beer. Furthest from real demand — hardest to predict.

4 Design Your Agent

This is the key decision. For each role you're assigned, write instructions that tell your AI agent how to order beer each round. There are no presets — you design the strategy from scratch.

Your Role: Retailer — Design Your Agent

Write instructions that tell your AI agent how to decide order quantities each round.

Some things to think about when writing your strategy:

📦Should you order exactly what's demanded, or add a buffer? ⏳Shipments take 2 weeks — how do you account for the delay? 📈What if demand suddenly changes? React fast or stay steady? 💰Holding stock costs €0.50/unit — backlog costs €1.00/unit. Which risk is worse?
Think carefully: Your strategy runs automatically for every round. You can't change it once the game starts. What sounds smart on paper might fail in practice — that's the point of the experiment!

6 Watch Your Agent Play

The game runs automatically. Each round, your AI agent sees the current state — demand, inventory, backlog, pipeline — and decides how much to order based on your strategy.

You'll see reasoning bubbles showing what the agent is thinking:

Round 5 — Agent Decision
Retailer Your Agent
AI Reasoning
"Demand jumped from 4 to 8 this round. I have 6 units in stock and 4 incoming. If I order 8, I'll have enough to cover next round's demand with a small buffer. Ordering more would build unnecessary inventory."
📦 Order: 8 units

Trucks animate across the game board as deliveries move through the supply chain. Watch how orders amplify as they travel upstream — that's the Bullwhip Effect in action.

7

7 The Goal

Keep costs low. Every unit sitting in your warehouse costs €0.50/week. Every unit of backlog costs €1.00/week.

The best strategy balances responsiveness with stability. Over-reacting to demand changes creates the very chaos you're trying to avoid.

🏆
Best Team & Best Player
Lowest cumulative cost wins. Only scored rounds count (warmup rounds are excluded).
The twist: You designed the strategy — but you can't intervene once the game starts. Did your instructions cover edge cases? Did you account for the lead time? That's what makes this interesting.
